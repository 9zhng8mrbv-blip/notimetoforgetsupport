Frequently Asked Questions
What's the difference between To-Do List and What's Due?
To-Do List tasks remind you a set time after you create them — perfect for things you need a nudge about. What's Due tasks have a specific deadline, and you can set multiple advance reminders before the due date.
How do I connect Google or Microsoft calendars?
Go to your iPhone's Settings → Calendar → Accounts → Add Account, then sign in with Google or Microsoft. Once added, return to the app's Calendar tab and your events will appear automatically. You can then import any event as a task with one tap.
How do repeating tasks work?
When you mark a repeating task as done, it stays completed for the rest of the day. The next morning, it automatically resets as a fresh active task with the date advanced by your chosen interval (daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly, every 6 months, or annually).
Can I break a task into smaller steps?
Yes! Every task can have subtasks. Add them when creating a task, or tap the steps chip on any card to expand it and add more. When all subtasks are checked off, the parent task completes automatically.
How do medicine reminders work?
Add a medicine with its dosage, frequency, and schedule times. You'll receive push notifications at each dose time. You can also set a refill date and the app will remind you 3 days before you run out.
I'm not receiving notifications. What should I do?
Go to Settings → Notifications → No Time to Forget on your iPhone and make sure notifications are enabled with banners, sounds, and badges turned on. Also check that Do Not Disturb or Focus modes aren't silencing alerts.
Is my data stored in the cloud?
No. All your tasks, medicines, and settings are stored locally on your device. Nothing is uploaded to any server. Your data stays entirely on your phone.

Privacy Policy

Your privacy matters

No Time to Forget is designed with your privacy at its core. Here's the simple version:

  • All data is stored locally on your device
  • We do not collect any personal information
  • We do not use analytics or tracking
  • We do not share data with third parties
  • No account or sign-in is required
  • Calendar access is read-only and stays on-device
  • Notifications are scheduled locally, not through external servers

The app requests only two system permissions: Calendar access (to display and import your events) and Notification access (to send local reminders). Both are optional and can be revoked at any time in your iPhone's Settings.

If you delete the app, all data is permanently removed from your device.
